The only surviving version of this short, apocalyptic prophecy in verse, can be found in MS Carpentras .336, Fol. 200v. The manuscript is usually dated 1405-7.

O Babilònia, tu Barcelona!

  1. O Babilònia, tu Barcelona!
  2. En tu florint l’arbre dela supèrbia,
  3. serà son fruit la jornada cella
  4. si, penedint, Dèu beneit no et perdona.
  5. Mostrar t’ha Dèu alguns horribles signes.
  6. Semblant farà a les tres veïnes
  7. Del teu comtat: per mal e per roïnes
  8. Les confondrà—e d’aço són dignes.
  9. Dèu poderós tot penedir espera.
  10. Penedint-te d’ell hauràs indulgència.
  11. Si que fas ta alta eminència,
  12. Si trametrà com Sodoma la fera.
  13. Ton mal vendrà de març o de setembre,
  14. A mitja nit, tu estant adormida:
  15. Per llamps e trons seràs espaordida.
  16. Vulles-te, donques, per penitència rembre.

Oh Barcelona you Babylon!

  1. Oh Barcelona you Babylon!
  2. In you blooms the tree of pride,
  3. It will bear its fruit that day
  4. If, regretful, blessed God does not forgive you.
  5. God has shown you some horrible portents.
  6. He will do the same to the three neighbours
  7. Of your county: with evil and ruin
  8. he will confound them—they deserve the steel.
  9. All repentance waits on mighty God.
  10. By repenting of him you will have indulgence.
  11. If you make yourself a great eminence,
  12. He will send the beast as he did with Sodom.
  13. Your fall will come in March or September,
  14. At midnight, you being asleep
  15. by lightning and thunder you will be terrified.
  16. Wish then to be redeemed by penitence.

Commentary

  • 4-The meaning here is not entirely clear. The Diccionari català-valencià-balear defines ‘pendir-se’ as ‘A algú, saber-li greu d’haver fet o deixat de fer alguna cosa’ and as deriving from the Latin ‘poeniteo’. The sense of the word as having to do with repentance is evident from its other uses in the poem. However, we would not expect God to be repentant and there is no obvious alternative for who might be the subject of the verb, therefore I have opted for ‘regretful’ in the sense of God being regretful of his creation and deciding not to forgive Barcelona/Babylon and instead destroy it.
  • 5- See Revelation 12 and 13 for the apocalyptic vocabulary at work here, including reference to ‘signs’ and ‘the beast’.
